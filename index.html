Background

I am currently a graphic design student at TCC at the Visual Art and Design Center. Growing up in a very art centric household, I grew a passion for the arts through drawing, painting, photography, and now design. Surrounded by my siblings who are graphic designers, it was just in my blood to have a love for art and design. As a student, I have learned and grown fond of creating logos, making posters, and using fun typography. I love using Adobe Illustrator and have dabbled into After Effects, which is a program I was originally scared of, but have loved using. I want to spread my creativity and produce meaningful and fulfilling designs for clients that support their goals and their branding. One day I would just love to see something I have designed on the shelves or as a poster outside. I am excited to see how I grow as a designer and how design evolves.

Goals

As a graphic designer, there are many things I want to accomplish in my career. Going into it, one of my biggest goals is to work for either a movie or a video game company to work on their marketing and designs for posters and advertising. One of my inspirations is Saul Bass and his work with movie posters. He had accomplished the perfect look for very minimal, yet impactful posters in his career. I would also would love to work for Sony since they have a vast selection for opportunity -- they make video games, movies, cameras, etc so there is definitely a place for design. However, with learning design in general, I have loved creating logos and brand identities. I aspire to work with a design firm such as Pentagram to be able to collaborate with major brands. And even if it isn’t a major brand, I would just love to be on a project that involves creating a brand identity or a rebranding. In such a progressive and evolving world, I feel like there is always a need to create something for someone whether it is an individual, a company, or a production and I would love to have my own creative input to take part in changes or new things.
